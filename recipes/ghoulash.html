Southern Ghoulash

Description

Southern Ghoulash is a hearty one-pot meal the whole family will enjoy. It’s a quick and relatively easy dish to prepare with a hearty tomato meat sauce and pasta.
This type of ghoulash has been very popular in the south for many years and I think everyone makes it a little differently. The common ingredients are pasta, ground beef, and tomato sauce, and many recipes call for cheddar cheese. I also like to add corn to mine, but that’s not as typical. You'll find a detailed ingredient list and step-by-step instructions in the recipe below

Ingredients

This ghoulash recipe is incredibly customizable, so you can easily alter the ingredient list to suit your needs.
    If you want to stay true to the original recipe, though, these are the ingredients you'll need to add to your grocery list:
  • 2 pounds lean ground beef
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1/2 red or green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 (28-ounce) can Tomato Sauce
  • 1 (28-ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon seasoned salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 cups uncooked elbow pasta
  • 1 (11-ounce) can corn, drained
  • Shredded Cheddar cheese and sour cream for serving

Steps

How to Make Southern Ghoulash

  1. Heat a lightly greased Dutch oven over medium heat and add the ground beef, onion, and bell pepper.
    Break meat apart with a wooden spoon as it cooks.
  2. Once meat is no longer pink, drain off any liquid/grease.
    Return Dutch oven to heat and add garlic and red pepper flakes.
    Cook 1 minute.
  3. Add tomato sauce, diced tomatoes, Worcestershire sauce, sugar, Italian seasoning, Cajun seasoning, seasoned salt, paprika, pepper and bay leaves.
    Simmer 5 minutes.
  4. Add water and pasta and simmer, covered, until pasta is cooked, about 15 minutes.
    Discard bay leaves.
  5. Stir in corn and serve with cheddar cheese and sour cream.
